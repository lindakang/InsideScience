Eli Lilly and Company (LLY), established in 1876, is a centennial global pharmaceutical powerhouse dedicated to developing and marketing medicines and vaccines across various therapeutic areas, including Alzheimer's disease, diabetes, oncology, metabolic disorders, and autoimmune diseases.

The company boasts a diversified product portfolio featuring market-leading drugs such as Trulicity (dulaglutide) for diabetes, Verzenio (abemaciclib) for breast cancer, and Taltz for psoriasis. However, its current growth is primarily driven by its blockbuster GLP-1 receptor agonists: Mounjaro (tirzepatide) and Zepbound (tirzepatide).

Lilly's current market valuation reflects strong investor confidence, largely due to its competitive advantage in the weight loss and type 2 diabetes markets. While the entry of competitors beyond Novo Nordisk poses a challenge to maintaining market share over the next three years, Lilly is aggressively expanding its pipeline beyond weight management.

History and Key Milestones

Eli Lilly was founded in 1876 by Colonel Eli Lilly, a pharmaceutical chemist and Civil War veteran. After serving in the Union Army (1861–1865), he worked in several pharmacies before establishing his own manufacturing business.

Early Development and Innovation (1876 – 1940s)

  • 1876: Colonel Eli Lilly founded the company after a brief partnership with John F. Johnston. The initial focus was on high-quality drugs, including quinine for malaria.
  • 1880s: Introduced innovations such as gelatin-coated pills/capsules and fruit-flavored or sugar-coated tablets for easier ingestion.
  • 1923: Commercialization of Insulin. In collaboration with the University of Toronto, Lilly became the first company to mass-produce insulin (Iletin), establishing its leadership in diabetes care.
  • 1940s: Pioneered the mass production of penicillin for Allied forces during World War II.

Post-War Expansion (1950s – 1980s)

  • 1952: Became a publicly traded company.
  • 1954: Entered animal health by establishing Elanco Products Company.
  • 1955: Became one of the first manufacturers of the Salk polio vaccine.
  • 1961: Developed its first oncology drug.
  • 1971: Acquired Elizabeth Arden (cosmetics), later sold in 1987.
  • 1977: Expanded into medical devices by acquiring IVAC and Cardiac Pacemakers.

Biotech Rise and Neuroscience Breakthroughs (1990s – Present)

  • 1982: Launched Humulin® (human insulin), becoming one of the first companies to use recombinant DNA technology, cementing its diabetes leadership.
  • 1986/1987: Launched Prozac® (fluoxetine). This selective serotonin reuptake inhibitor (SSRI) revolutionized depression treatment and established Lilly as a major player in neuroscience.

Modern Challenges and Transformation (2000s – Present)

  • 2011: Formed an alliance with Boehringer Ingelheim to develop diabetes drugs, including Jardiance®.
  • 2014: Acquired Novartis’ animal health business to strengthen Elanco (fully spun off in 2019).
